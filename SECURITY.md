# Security Policy

## Reporting a Vulnerability

We take security seriously. If you discover a security vulnerability in any
Microconsortium project, please report it responsibly.

### How to report

**Email:** security@microconsortium.com

For sensitive disclosures, please encrypt your report with our PGP key:
- **Fingerprint:** *(будет добавлен после генерации PGP-ключа)*
- **Public key:** https://microconsortium.com/pgp.asc

Alternatively, you can use [GitHub's private vulnerability reporting](https://docs.github.com/en/code-security/security-advisories/guidance-on-reporting-and-writing-information-about-vulnerabilities/privately-reporting-a-security-vulnerability)
on individual repositories.

### What to include

- Affected project and version(s)
- Steps to reproduce or proof-of-concept
- Potential impact assessment
- Your suggested fix, if any

### Our commitment

- We will acknowledge receipt within **48 hours**
- We will provide an initial assessment within **7 days**
- We will keep you informed of progress through resolution
- We will credit you in the security advisory unless you prefer anonymity
- We will coordinate disclosure timing with you

### Scope

This policy applies to:
- All repositories under https://github.com/microconsortium
- The microconsortium.com domain and its subdomains

Out of scope:
- Third-party services we don't control
- Issues in dependencies (please report upstream)
- Social engineering attacks

### Safe harbor

We support security research conducted in good faith. We will not pursue legal
action against researchers who:
- Make a good-faith effort to comply with this policy
- Report vulnerabilities promptly
- Avoid privacy violations and service disruption
- Do not exploit vulnerabilities beyond what's necessary to demonstrate them

Thank you for helping keep Microconsortium and our users secure.
